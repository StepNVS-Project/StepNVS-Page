StepNVS:
Taming Video Diffusion Model for One-Step Driving Novel View Synthesis

StepNVS generates high-fidelity novel views in one step.

Left: Compared to multi-step baseline methods that rely on iterative denoising, our approach efficiently generates the novel view in one step.
Right: Compared to multi-step baselines that follow long, curved denoising trajectories, StepNVS reaches the target prediction directly in a single step.

Novel Trajectory Synthesis

We synthesize novel views by applying lateral shifts along the Y-axis. From left to right, the views transition smoothly from -2m and -1m left shifts, through the Ground Truth (Center), to +1m and +2m right shifts.

Surround-View Synthesis

We generate consistent 6-camera surround-view sequences across various lateral shifts, maintaining temporal stability and visual coherence.

Free Trajectory Control

StepNVS also allow for diverse control over forward/backward (X-axis) and vertical (Z-axis) camera movements. From left to right, we show the Original Trajectory, Backward 1m, Forward 1m, Down 1m, and Up 1m.

Bullet-Time View Synthesis

We create "bullet-time" rendering effects by seamlessly altering the viewpoint while maintaining a fixed timestamp.

Comparison with Baseline Methods

Qualitative Comparison across Different Scenes. Our method preserves clearer scene structures and better visual quality compare with the reconstruction-based and multi-step methods across diverse driving scenarios.
Comparison on Novel Trajectory Synthesis. We visualize the synthesized views under laterally shifted trajectories (1m/2m/4m). Our one-step method generates high-quality details for road elements, including lane markings and surrounding vehicles, while reconstruction-based methods suffer from severe artifacts, and multi-step methods require iterative denoising to achieve comparable results.
